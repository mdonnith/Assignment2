Rooted in the Wild

The Forager & The Maker

Chelsea’s hands have always been in the dirt. Originally from the towering forests of Oregon, her journey took her through the rugged landscapes of Alaska before she finally found her "forever home" in the heart of Easthampton, Massachusetts. With a degree in Environmental Science, Chelsea has spent her life studying the intricate balance of the natural world—a perspective that now flows directly into her clay.

Flora Form was born from these wanderings. Whether she’s hiking the local peaks or scouting for mushrooms and wildflowers with her husband, John, and their faithful canine muse, Flora, Chelsea is constantly gathering inspiration. Her "happy place" is found in the small, often-overlooked details: the curve of a dried stem, the texture of moss on stone, or the perfect symmetry of a woodland bud.

After a lifetime of exploring the wilderness, Chelsea is now bridging the gap between the forest floor and your home. Flora Form creates small-batch pottery designed specifically to hold those tiny, found treasures—the little pieces of nature that remind us to slow down, look closer, and breathe.
